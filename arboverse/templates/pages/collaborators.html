Prof. Pablo Murcia, PhD

University of Glasgow, UK

Prof. Pablo Murcia, PhD

Professor of Integrative Virology at Centre for Virus Research, University of Glasgow, UK. His main research interest is to understand the molecular and evolutionary mechanisms that underpin viral host switching and viral emergence. Dr. Murcia is a collaborator of Arboverse in Virus Discovery studies.

Prof. Nuno Faria, PhD

Imperial College London, UK

Prof. Nuno Faria, PhD

Reader in the Department for Infectious Disease Epidemiology at Imperial College London, UK. He is interested in the epidemiology and evolution of rapidly evolving viruses that circulate in human, vector and animal populations, particularly arboviruses and SARS-CoV-2. Dr. Faria is a collaborator in arboviruses epidemiology and SARS-CoV-2 response in Brazil.

Prof. José Luiz Proença-Modena, PhD

Prof. José Luiz Proença-Modena, PhD

Assistant Professor at the University of Campinas (Brazil) in the Department of Genetics, Evolution, Microbiology and Immunology. His research interests revolve pathogenic mechanisms of arboviruses and emerging respiratory viruses. Dr. Proença-Modena is a partner with pathogenesis, epidemiology and SARS-CoV-2 studies.

Robert Gifford, PhD

University of Glasgow, UK

Robert Gifford, PhD

Evolutionary Biologist at the MRC-University of Glasgow Centre for Virus Research. He is research is the development of theories that link our growing understanding of contemporary viruses with ecological and evolutionary phenomena that took place over timescales ranging from hundreds to millions of years.

Prof. Ester Sabino, MD, PhD

Jean Paul Carrera

University of Oxford, UK | Instituto Gorgas, Panama

Jean Paul Carrera

Mr Carrera is a PhD student at the University of Oxford (UK) in the Department of Zoology. He is research focuses on understanding the transmission dynamics of enzootic and epidemic arthropod-borne-zoonotic viruses in Latin America, on deciphering the factors that lead to their emergence and adaptation in new environments. Mr Carrera is a partner in ecology and epidemiology studies of arboviruses.
