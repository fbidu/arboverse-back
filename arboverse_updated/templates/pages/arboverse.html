Expansion of Aedes-borne virus transmission risk with climate change

Forecasting of the impacts of climate change on Aedes-borne viruses

Fuction

Displays global expansion and redistribution of Aedes-borne virus transmission risk with climate change

Resolution

5-minute spatial resolution (~10 km2 at the equator)

Geographic Coverage

Globally

Frequency of updates

Not updated

Date of content

Future scenarios for 2050 and 2080 using the RCP (Representative Concentration Pathway) 2.6 and RCP 8.5 in HadGEM2-ES model.

License
CC BY 4.0
Overview

This dataset shows the number of months per year when disease transmission by Aedes-borne virus is possible under baseline conditions using climate data from 1960-1990, as well as in 2050 and 2080. In addition to the baseline temperature suitability for Aedes-borne virus were used four climate change scenarios (RCP 2.6, RCP 4.5, RCP 6.0, and RCP 8.5) were to predict where temperatures will be suitable for mosquitoes to thrive in 2050 and 2080. The ARBOVERSE presents the maps only for RCP 2.6 and RCP 8.5. For more methodological details and limitations, please see the original source.

Köppen-Geiger classification

Present and future Köppen-Geiger climate classification

Fuction

Displays present and future Köppen-Geiger climate classification

Resolution

0.0083° spatial resolution (~1 km2 at the equator)

Geographic Coverage

Globally

Frequency of updates

Not updated

Date of content

Köppen-Geiger climate classification of present-day (1980–2016) and for projected future conditions (2071–2100) under climate change from an ensemble of 32 climate model projections (RCP 8.5).

License
CC BY 4.0
Overview

This dataset shows the Köppen-Geiger climate classification for the present-day (1980–2016) and projected future scenario (2071–2100) under climate change. The Köppen climate classification scheme includes 29 subclassifications of five main climate groups, which are tropical, arid, temperate, continental, and polar. The present-day map was derived from an ensemble of four high-resolution, topographically-corrected climatic maps. The future map was derived from an ensemble of 32 climate model projections (RCP 8.5), by superimposing the projected climate change anomaly on the baseline high-resolution climatic maps. For more methodological details and limitations, please see the original source.

Resolution

Globally

Frequency of updates

License

Overview

This dataset is the aridity index calculated as the ratio of mean annual precipitation to mean annual potential evapotranspiration, using data from the WorldClim Global Climate Database. The aridity index is influenced by several factors, including solar radiation, temperature, air humidity, and wind speed. Other contributing factors include the leaf area index of an area, as water pooling on top of leaves and tree canopies can lead to faster evaporation than if precipitating water entered the soil layer. For more methodological details and limitations, please see the original source.

Vulnerability to drought and floods

Show the vulnerability to floods and droughts

Fuction

Displays the level of vulnerability of groundwater resources of the earth. It presents the intrinsic vulnerability of groundwater systems and the sensitivity or resistance of those systems to natural disasters

Resolution

Not described

Geographic Coverage

Globally

Frequency of updates

Not updated

Date of content

2016-Present

License
CC BY-SA 3.0 IGO
Overview

This dataset presents a global overview of groundwater vulnerability classification. The authors developed a scale called “Total Vulnerability Range” (TVR), which is divided into three categories: low, moderate, and high groundwater vulnerability. Generally, the lower the numerical score the lower the vulnerability and the higher the level of natural protection of groundwater against hydro-climatic disasters. Finally, the TVR for floods and the TVR for droughts have been combined. The resulting categories of vulnerability are depicted on the map in different colors. For more methodological details and limitations, please see the original source.

Overview

This dataset shows the ecoregions represent geographically distinct assemblages of biodiversity whose boundaries encompass the space required to sustain key ecological processes. Biomes represent major ecological communities of organisms adapted to a particular climatic or environmental condition in a large geographic area where they occur. This map is based upon more than 100 years of biogeographic scholarship, including extensive field investigations. For this reason, ecoregions reflect the on-the-ground distribution of regional biotas differently than biogeographic units derived from parametric approaches. Overview

This dataset shows the ecoregions represent geographically distinct assemblages of biodiversity whose boundaries encompass the space required to sustain key ecological processes. (1) Half Protected: More than 50% of the total ecoregion area is protected. (2) Nature Could Reach Half: Less than 50% of the total ecoregion area is protected but the sum of total ecoregion protected and unprotected natural habitat remaining is more than 50%. Ecoregions in this category have enough remaining natural habitat to reach Half Protected if additional protected areas or other types of conservation areas are added to the system. (3) Nature Could Recover: The sum of the amount of natural habitat remaining and the amount of the total ecoregion that is protected is less than 50% but more than 20%. Ecoregions in this category would require restoration to reach Half Protected because the amount of available habitat outside protected areas plus the existing protected areas is below 50%. (4) Nature Imperiled: The sum of the amount of natural habitat remaining and the amount of the total ecoregion that is protected is less than or equal to 20%. Overview

This dataset presents the Gridded Population of the World, which consists of estimates of human population density (number of persons per square kilometer) based on counts consistent with national censuses and population registers for 2000, 2005, 2010, 2015, and 2020. A proportional allocation gridding algorithm, utilizing approximately 13.5 million national and sub-national administrative units, was used to assign population counts to 30 arc-second grid cells. Overview

This dataset presents the Gross Domestic Product (GDP) per capita as a key economic indicator that measures the monetary value of goods and services provided per person. To create a high-resolution, complete picture of GDP per capita, the author calculated it using the World Bank Development Indicators database.
